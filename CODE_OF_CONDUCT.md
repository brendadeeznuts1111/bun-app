# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Use welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
- **Gracefully accept constructive criticism**
- **Focus on what is best for the community**
- **Show empathy towards other community members**
- **Be patient with newcomers and questions**
- **Provide helpful and constructive feedback**

### Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassing, abusive, or discriminatory language**
- **Personal attacks or insults**
- **Public or private harassment**
- **Publishing others' private information without permission**
- **Spamming or trolling**
- **Disruptive behavior**
- **Sexually explicit or violent material**
- **Threats of violence or harm**
- **Creating a hostile or intimidating environment**

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### Community Leaders

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Scope

This code of conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement

### Reporting Guidelines

If you witness or are subjected to unacceptable behavior, please report it through one of these channels:

1. **GitHub Issues**: Create a private issue mentioning `@brendadeeznuts1111`
2. **Email**: Contact the project maintainer directly
3. **GitHub Report**: Use GitHub's reporting features for content violations

### What to Include in Your Report

- **Description**: What happened
- **People involved**: Who was affected
- **Context**: When and where it occurred
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, links, or other documentation
- **Impact**: How it affected you or others

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: We'll acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: We'll review the report thoroughly
3. **Action**: We'll take appropriate action based on severity
4. **Follow-up**: We'll inform you of the resolution

### Consequences

Community leaders may take any action they deem appropriate, including but not limited to:

- **Warning**: Private warning about inappropriate behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from community spaces
- **Content Removal**: Removal of inappropriate content
- **Reporting**: Reporting to platform administrators if necessary

## Conflict Resolution

### Disagreements

We understand that disagreements happen. When they do:

1. **Assume good faith** from others
2. **Focus on issues**, not people
3. **Listen to different perspectives**
4. **Seek common ground**
5. **Ask for mediation** if needed

### Mediation

If you need help resolving a conflict:

- Contact a community leader for mediation
- Request a private discussion space
- Use GitHub's private issue feature for sensitive topics

## Positive Contributions

### What We Encourage

- **Helpful responses** to questions
- **Constructive feedback** on contributions
- **Mentoring** of new contributors
- **Documentation improvements**
- **Bug reports and feature requests**
- **Code reviews and testing**
- **Community support and engagement**

### Recognition

We recognize and appreciate positive contributions through:

- **Mentions in release notes**
- **Contributor lists in documentation**
- **GitHub badges and achievements**
- **Community spotlights**
- **Thank you messages and appreciation**

We are committed to making our community accessible to everyone, including:

- **People with disabilities**
- **Non-native English speakers**
- **Beginners and experienced developers**
- **People from diverse backgrounds**
- **Different time zones and schedules**

### Accommodations

We provide accommodations such as:

- **Alternative communication methods**
- **Flexible meeting times**
- **Language assistance**
- **Technical support**
- **Documentation in multiple formats**

## Professional Conduct

### Technical Discussions

When discussing technical topics:

- **Stay on topic** and relevant to the project
- **Provide evidence** for technical claims
- **Be open to different approaches**
- **Acknowledge limitations** in your knowledge
- **Cite sources** when appropriate

### Code Reviews

When reviewing code:

- **Be constructive** and specific
- **Explain reasoning** behind suggestions
- **Recognize effort** and good work
- **Offer to help** with improvements
- **Follow project guidelines**

## Privacy and Confidentiality

### Personal Information

- **Do not share** others' personal information
- **Respect privacy settings** and preferences
- **Ask permission** before sharing screenshots or logs
- **Remove sensitive data** from public posts

### Project Information

- **Respect embargoes** and release schedules
- **Protect security vulnerabilities** until disclosure
- **Follow responsible disclosure** practices

## Cultural Sensitivity

### Global Community

We are a global community with diverse:

- **Cultural backgrounds**
- **Time zones**
- **Holidays and traditions**
- **Communication styles**
- **Technical environments**

### Guidelines

- **Be patient** with language differences
- **Respect cultural holidays** and schedules
- **Consider time zones** in communications
- **Avoid culturally specific references**
- **Be inclusive** in examples and documentation

## Consequences for Violations

### Severity Levels

**Minor Violations**:
- First offense: Private warning
- Second offense: Temporary suspension (24 hours)
- Third offense: Temporary suspension (1 week)

**Moderate Violations**:
- First offense: Temporary suspension (1 week)
- Second offense: Temporary suspension (1 month)
- Third offense: Permanent ban

**Severe Violations**:
- Immediate permanent ban
- Report to platform administrators
- Contact law enforcement if necessary

### Appeals Process

If you believe action was taken unfairly:

1. **Submit appeal** to project maintainers
2. **Provide evidence** and context
3. **Review by different community leaders**
4. **Decision within 7 days**
5. **Final decision** is binding
